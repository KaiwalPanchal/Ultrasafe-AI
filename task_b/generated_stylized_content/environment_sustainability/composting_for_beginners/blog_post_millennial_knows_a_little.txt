### Greens vs Browns: The Perfect Duo

- **Green materials:** These are your high-nitrogen goodies like veggie scraps, coffee grounds, and fresh grass clippings. They‚Äôre like protein shakes for microbes.
- **Brown materials:** Dry leaves, cardboard, paper, and wood chips. These provide carbon ‚Äî basically the energy and structure the microbes jam to.

## Pro Tips From the Compost Pros

- **Layer up smart:** Alternate greens and browns to keep that C:N ratio just right.
- **Turn, turn, turn:** Aerate your pile every 1-2 weeks to speed things up.
- **Check moisture:** Compost should feel like a wrung-out sponge‚Äîtoo dry, microbes nap; too wet, things get smelly.
- **Try compost tea:** This liquid gold can be sprayed on plants or poured into soil as a nutrient boost.
- **Diversity rocks:** More types of organic materials mean a richer, healthier compost final product.

## Ready to Start? Here‚Äôs Your Action Plan

1. **Pick your system:** Choose a compost bin or set up a compost pile that fits your space.

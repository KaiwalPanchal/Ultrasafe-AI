## The Composting 101: Breaking It Down

### What Goes in Your Compost?

- **Green Stuff:** This is the juicy, nitrogen-rich crew ‚Äî veggie scraps, coffee grounds, and fresh grass clippings. They‚Äôre the food for the microbes breaking things down.  
- **Brown Stuff:** These are carbon-rich materials like dried leaves, cardboard, and paper ‚Äî kinda like the snacks that give microbes energy and structure.  

The sweet spot? Mix greens and browns at about 25-30 parts brown to 1 part green (this is called the carbon-to-nitrogen ratio, but no need to stress about it ‚Äî just remember: balance is key).

### Oxygen is Your BFF

Your compost pile needs air. Microbes doing their magic love oxygen ‚Äî they‚Äôre called *aerobic* microbes. If your pile doesn‚Äôt get enough air, it turns anaerobic and starts making stinky methane instead (eww). So, turning your pile every week or two keeps things fresh and fast.

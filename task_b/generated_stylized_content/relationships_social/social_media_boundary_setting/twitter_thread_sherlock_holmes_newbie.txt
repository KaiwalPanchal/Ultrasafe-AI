2/8 Observe closely: Social media boundary setting means choosing and sharing your limits when interacting online—who may see your posts, how often you engage, and what you share. These invisible walls guard your peace and privacy. Elementary, yet essential.

3/8 Consider the numbers: As of January 2025, nearly 4.9 billion souls roam social media’s vast streets—over 60% of the world! Yet, a 2022 study found 62% suffer stress from unclear boundaries. What do we infer from this troubling detail?

4/8 Important clue: 81% agree setting personal boundaries on social media reduces quarrels. The APA confirms boundary violations raise anxiety and depression. Those who manage privacy report 30% more satisfaction online. The pattern is clear.

5/8 Now, the core concepts:  
- Personal boundaries = your line on acceptable behavior online.  
- Privacy management = who views, messages, or tags you.  
- Emotional boundaries = shielding your mind from toxicity.  
- Time boundaries = how long you journey down the digital rabbit hole.  
- Public vs. private posts = knowing who sees what.

6/8 Practical cases illuminate the theory:  
Jane protects her Instagram with “close friends” lists. Michael restricts late-night chats, toggling “Do Not Disturb.” Anika schedules daily digital detoxes. And a couple agrees to keep conflicts off their public page. Small steps, great impact.

7/8 Beware common misconceptions: boundaries aren’t walls of hostility but clear fences of respect. Privacy tools alone don’t suffice without vigilant management. Everyone benefits—whether a casual passerby or a devoted user. And remember, boundaries must be spoken, not assumed.

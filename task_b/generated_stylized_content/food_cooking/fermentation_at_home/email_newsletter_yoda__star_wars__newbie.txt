Turning sugars and carbs into acids, gases, or alcohol, microbes like bacteria, yeast, and molds do. Preserve food, improve flavor, and boost nutrition, fermentation does. Kombucha, kimchi, and yogurt, popular examples they are — different microbes and methods each used.

**Microbes and Methods, Know You Should**  

- Kombucha a fermented tea is, made by SCOBY — symbiotic bacteria and yeast culture, hmmm. About 7 to 14 days ferment it takes, warm temperature needed, 20 to 30°C best. Millions to hundreds of millions probiotics you get in each sip (10^6 to 10^8 CFU/mL, Marsh et al., 2014).  
- Kimchi, Korean fermented veggies like cabbage and radish it is. Lactic acid bacteria, such as Lactobacillus kimchii, transform sugars into acids. Cooler temps needed, 0 to 10°C for 1 to 4 weeks. Billions of microbes it holds (10^7 to 10^9 CFU/g, Lee et al., 2016).  
- Yogurt comes from milk fermented by Lactobacillus delbrueckii bulgaricus and Streptococcus thermophilus. Lactose changes into lactic acid, giving taste and texture. Warmth of 40-45°C for 4 to 8 hours needed. Probiotics many, 10^7 to 10^9 CFU/g usually found (FAO/WHO, 2001).  

Learn now, these microbes make vitamins and helpful probiotics for gut health, they do.

**Ferment Right, You Must**  

- Sweetened tea kombucha uses, with SCOBY and starter liquid it mixes. Covered jar with cloth, breathe it must, ferment patiently for days. Flavor with fruit or herbs, you can.  
- Kimchi begins by salting vegetables, pulling water out it does. Then mix with spice paste—garlic, ginger, chili powder, fish sauce—packed tight to ferment coolly. Sour and strong flavor after weeks develop.  
- Yogurt, pasteurize milk first, cool to about 43°C. Then add live cultures, warmth keep for several hours until thickening occurs. Strain or flavor afterward, as you wish.  

Patience and care keep safe your fermentations. Sanitize containers, clean tools always—prevent spoiled food and bad microbes, you will.

**Misconceptions, Clear Them We Must**  

- Unsafe without fridge, fermentation is not when done clean and right. Starter cultures protect you.  
- All probiotics survive not through storage or stomach, strain and product matter.  
- Alcoholic all fermented foods are not; kombucha has tiny, about 0.5% alcohol, kimchi and yogurt much less, almost none.  
- Sour all fermentation flavors are not; kombucha can be sweet and fizzy.  
- SCOBY keep forever you need not; start new batches from commercial kombucha if you like, but contamination risks rise.  

Keep these truths close.

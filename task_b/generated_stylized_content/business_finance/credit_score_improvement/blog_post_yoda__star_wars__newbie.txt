## 2. Key Facts, Know Them You Must

- **Credit Score Ranges, Important They Are** (FICO® Score, widely used it is):  
  - Poor: 300–579, trouble you will find  
  - Fair: 580–669, beginner you are  
  - Good: 670–739, steady progress shown  
  - Very Good: 740–799, strong you are becoming  
  - Exceptional: 800–850, a master of credit you are  
  (Source: FICO, 2023, yes)

- Average score in 2024, Experian says it is about 714. Good overall, but room for growth, always there is.  
  (Source: Experian State of Credit, 2024)

- Most important, payment history is — 35% of your score, it accounts for. Pay on time, you must.  
  (Source: FICO)

- Others, amounts owed (30%), length of credit history (15%), new credit inquiries (10%), credit mix (10%) are key parts too.

- Below 580, nearly 15% of Americans' scores are, risky that is. Care needed, yes.  
  (Source: CFPB Consumer Credit Panel)

## 3. Core Concepts, Simple to Understand

### a. What Builds a Credit Score?

- **Payment History:** Pay on time, trustworthy you’ll seem. Miss payments, danger there is.  
- **Credit Utilization:** Use only small part of credit you have, less than 30% you should. Balance low, good that is.  
- **Length of Credit History:** Old accounts help you. Longer history, better score it makes.  
- **Types of Credit:** Mix it, like loans, credit cards, mortgages. Variety, a good thing it is.  
- **New Credit:** Too many accounts opened fast, hurt your score it can.

### b. Credit Reports, Check Them You Must

From three wise bureaus — Equifax, Experian, TransUnion — your credit reports come. Check them for errors you should, yes. Mistakes or fraud, find them you must, correct them fast.

### c. How to Improve?

Use credit wisely, consistently you must. Remove bad marks over time, manage balances and new requests carefully, yes.

## 5. Misconceptions, Clear Them We Will

- **Check your own credit and score drop, no.** Soft inquiry it is, like looking in mirror, harmless.  
- **Close old accounts and score rise? No!** Credit limit shrinks, utilization rises, score falls.  
- **Pay off debt and instant boost? Patience, young one.** Consistent good habits needed, time to reflect score improvements it takes.  
- **Income affects score? Nonsense it is.** Behavior with credit, only focus credit scores have.

## 8. Action Steps, Follow Them Carefully, You Should

1. **Obtain Credit Reports:** Equifax, Experian, TransUnion…free yearly reports ask you must.  
2. **Dispute Errors:** Find mistakes? Challenge and correct quickly.  
3. **Pay Bills on Time:** Timely payments, your biggest ally.  
4. **Keep Balances Low:** Under 30%, better under 10%, your credit use should be.  
5. **Avoid Many New Accounts:** Space out applications. Hard inquiries hurt score, yes.  
6. **Keep Old Accounts Open:** Preserve history long.  
7. **Diversify Your Credit:** Use different kinds wisely.  
8. **Try Secured Cards if Needed:** Build or rebuild credit, step by step.  
9. **Automate Payments:** Miss no dates, avoid penalties.  
10. **Monitor and Learn:** Watch your reports, protect from fraud.

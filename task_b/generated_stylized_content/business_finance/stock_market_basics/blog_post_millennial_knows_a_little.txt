## Breaking Down the Basics: Shares and Dividends, Explained

### Shares 101

Think of shares as digital tickets that prove you own a slice of a company‚Äôs pie. With those tickets, you might get voting rights to weigh in on company stuff, plus a claim on profits.

- **Market price vs intrinsic value:** Market price is what you pay when you buy shares right now. Intrinsic value is more like your personal estimate of what the company is really worth based on its fundamentals (kind of like valuing your favorite sneaker based on condition and hype, not just market price).
- **Liquidity matters:** Stocks can be bought or sold quickly on exchanges, making them easier to convert to cash compared to, say, real estate.

### Dividends Decoded

- Dividends are set by a company's board, usually showing up quarterly.
- **Dividend yield** tells you how much cash you get back relative to the stock price; **payout ratio** shows what slice of the company‚Äôs earnings go out as dividends.
- Some companies are dividend rockstars, like Johnson & Johnson ‚Äî which has increased its dividends for 61 years straight. Talk about consistency!

## Busting Some Stock Market Myths

- **Myth #1: Dividends are guaranteed.** Nope, companies can cut or pause dividends if finances get shaky.
- **Myth #2: Stocks always go up.** Stock prices can roller-coaster thanks to market shifts and company news.
- **Myth #3: You need to be rich to invest.** Anyone can start with a brokerage account, and some platforms let you invest with very little money.
- **Myth #4: Dividends are the only perk of shares.** Nope! Voting rights, price appreciation, and diversifying your portfolio also matter.

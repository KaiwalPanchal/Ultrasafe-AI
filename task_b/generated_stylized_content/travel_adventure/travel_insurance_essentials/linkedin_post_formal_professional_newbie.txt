I am pleased to share an important overview on travel insurance, an essential aspect of international travel that safeguards you from unexpected costs.

For those new to travel insurance, here is a quick overview: Travel insurance provides financial protection against risks such as medical emergencies, trip cancellations, lost luggage, and other travel-related issues. It is especially valuable when traveling abroad, where local insurance might not cover the full expenses.

Here are key points every beginner should understand:

• Medical emergencies abroad can be very expensive. For example, emergency evacuation may cost over $50,000 USD, typically covered by travel insurance.  
• Trip cancellations are the most common benefit claimed, making up over 40% of claims.  
• Younger travelers, including millennials and Gen Z, are more frequently buying travel insurance, with 60% of travelers under 35 having coverage.  
• Travel insurance costs roughly 4-10% of your total trip price — a small investment compared to potential losses.  
• Policies vary, covering items such as trip cancellation, medical treatment, baggage loss, and emergency evacuation. Understanding policy limits and exclusions is important.  

It’s important to start with a solid foundation by assessing your travel needs, comparing plans carefully, and purchasing coverage soon after booking your trip. Keeping your policy documents accessible and knowing how to file claims will help ensure smooth assistance when needed.

Please feel free to reach out with any questions as you explore this topic. Starting with these basics ensures your travel experience is safer and more secure.

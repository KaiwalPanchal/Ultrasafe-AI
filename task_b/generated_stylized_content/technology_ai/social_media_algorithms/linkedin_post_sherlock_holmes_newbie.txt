What mystery lies behind the scenes of your social media feed? Let us examine closely the curious case of social media algorithms—those invisible engines deciding what you see on platforms like Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube.

Observe how these algorithms sift through thousands of posts—Facebook, for example, reviews over 10,000 posts daily but shows you only about 300. What does this pattern reveal? By deduction, it is clear they analyse your interactions, content traits, and context to personalise your feed, aiming to keep you engaged. TikTok’s clever system learns swiftly from your viewing habits, while YouTube’s recommendations shape more than 70% of watch time. Notice the subtle detail: these algorithms balance signals like clicks, likes, and shares with fresh and diverse content to maintain your interest.

But beware misconceptions—these systems are neither random nor entirely neutral. They reflect platform goals and design choices, sometimes amplifying sensational or polarising content. Therefore, understanding their workings empowers you to navigate your digital world wisely.

**How to Sit, Properly**

Neutral spine, key concept it is. Natural curves keep, without slumping or arching too much. Chair height adjusted, feet flat on floor or footrest placed. Seat deep enough to support thighs, but not press behind knees. Lumbar support, preserve lower back curve it does. Desk height so forearms rest comfortably, parallel or slanting down. Top third of screen at eye level, neck flexion avoid you will.

Movement also important, yes. Not static sitting all day, you must do. Micro-movements and position changes, like tiny breaks, reduce fatigue and pressure on discs.

**Practical Ways to Use This Wisdom**

Sit-stand desks, helpful they are, allowing sitting and standing alternate you can. A 2018 study found reduced back pain after 12 weeks using these desks. At home, adjustable chairs, laptop risers, external keyboards help create ergonomic setups. Follow the 20-8-2 rule: 20 minutes sitting, 8 standing, 2 moving. Break static spine load, this does.

Training programs by companies like Microsoft and IBM teach posture care and remind to move, important supporting tools they are.

**Misconceptions to Banish, Hmmm**

Good posture is not sitting perfectly still for hours, no. Muscles tire, discomfort comes. Dynamic movement needed, yes. Standing desks alone fix not all—bad standing posture also cause pain.

Low back pain often from cumulative strain, not only injury. Ergonomic tools, use properly you must; without guidance, fixed posture they will not bring.

Hey friends! Ever wonder how to teach kids about money without turning it into a snooze fest? 🤔 Let’s break down the basics of financial literacy for kids—super easy, fun, and totally newbie-friendly. #MoneyTalks

2/8  
First up: what’s financial literacy? It’s just knowing how money works—earning, saving, spending, and even giving! Teaching this early helps kids grow up to dodge money stress later on. No sweat, you got this! 💸

3/8  
Here’s some eye-opening tea ☕: Only 22 US states require personal finance classes for high schoolers, and just 57% of Americans can answer basic money questions correctly. Bottom line? Starting young matters! 📚

4/8  
Let’s break it down by ages:  
- 3-5: Play store with pretend money  
- 6-8: Chores + allowance = earn & save  
- 9-12: Budgeting toys & needs vs wants  
- 13-18: How banks & credit cards work  
Simple steps make a huge difference!

5/8  
Pro tip: Using 3 jars labeled “Spend,” “Save,” and “Give” helps kids see where their money goes. It’s all about making choices they can actually understand—and feel good about. 🐷✨

6/8  
Don’t buy the myth that just giving allowance teaches money skills. Without guidance, it’s like giving a treasure map with no directions. Talk openly about money; kids actually want the scoop, and it boosts confidence!

7/8  
Tech alert 🚨: Apps like Greenlight and BusyKid make managing money fun and safe for kiddos. Plus, games like Financial Football teach money skills without boring lectures. Win-win, right?

8/8

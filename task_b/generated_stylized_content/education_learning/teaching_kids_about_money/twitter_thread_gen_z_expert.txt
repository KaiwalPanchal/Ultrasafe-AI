🔥 Let’s spill some tea on teaching kids about money — the REAL way to raise future financial bosses 💸🧵👇

1/ Lowkey, financial literacy for kids isn’t just “nice to have” — it’s a MUST. It’s all about equipping young peeps with the skills to make smart $$$ moves: earning, saving, budgeting, spending, and even giving. Fr, starting early = BIG brain move for their adult life.

2/ Spoiler alert: Only 22 U.S. states require high schoolers to take personal finance classes (Council for Economic Education, 2022). So yeah, the system lowkey drops the ball. That’s why parents & educators gotta step up. 💪

3/ Real talk — only 57% of Americans can handle basic finance questions (TIAA Institute-GFLEC, 2023). If adults struggle, imagine kids without a solid foundation. Early education is the plug for better money decisions later.

4/ Studies say kids who get those money lessons early are way more likely to save and budget effectively (NEFE research). And if they learn at home or school? They enter adulthood with more confidence handling cash (Journal of Consumer Affairs, 2021). Bet.

5/ Let’s break down the essentials every kid should know:  
- Money’s a tool to buy stuff (medium of exchange)  
- Work = $$$ (earning)  
- Save up for goals (saving)  
- Plan your $$$ flow (budgeting)  
- Needs vs wants (spending wisely)  
- Share the love (giving)  
- Banks & interest basics  
- Credit & debt 101 (for older kids)

6/ How to make it stick at different ages? Here’s the lowdown:  
3-5: Play store with toy money — kids learn buying & selling in a fun way.  
6-8: Introduce chores + allowance, split money into “Spend,” “Save,” “Give” jars.  
9-12: Budgeting for toys or activities — “How many weeks to save $50?” kinda stuff.  
13-18: Bank accounts, debit cards, & credit scores—teach that fancy adult money game.

7/ Common myths? Let’s bust ’em:  
- “Allowance = money skills” — nah, without guidance it’s just free cash (Cambridge study, 2019).  
- “Money talks stress kids out” — actually, talking early builds confidence (NFEC, 2020).  
- “Fin lit is just for adults” — habits build EARLY, no cap.  
- “School only handles it” — family vibes are huge for shaping money habits.

8/ Expert scoop: Dr. Lusardi says teaching compound interest early is a total game changer. Jump$tart Coalition pushes for age-appropriate, staged learning. Hands-on + talk-about approach = recipe for financial superhero vibes.

9/ Trends we’re vibing with now:  
- Kid-friendly apps like Greenlight & BusyKid teach digital $$ skills.  
- More states adding mandatory finance classes.  
- Gamified learning (Financial Football, Stock Market Game) making $$ lessons fun.  
- Focus on equity — programs for underserved families leveling the field.

10/ Real talk for parents: start money talks early with play & real cash. Use tech wisely, model good habits, keep it 100 with goals, and foster safe convos about wins & fails. For educators: weave finance into other classes, get families involved, keep lessons fresh. For policymakers: mandate, fund, research = the holy trinity.

11/ TL;DR: Teaching kids about money ain’t just “adulting prep,” it’s foundation-building for a lifetime of boss moves. With right timing, hands-on practice, and open convos, we’re setting up the next gen to OWN their financial futures. Let’s get that bread — responsibly. 🍞✨

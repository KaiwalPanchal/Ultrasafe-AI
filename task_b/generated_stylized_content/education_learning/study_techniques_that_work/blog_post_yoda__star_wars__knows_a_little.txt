## 2. Key Facts, You Must Know

- **Spacing Effect, Powerful It Is:** Spread study sessions over time, you should. Long-term memory, it strengthens more than cramming does. (Cepeda et al., 2006, *Psychological Science*)

- **Retrieval Practice, Remember You Will:** Recall actively, not just reread. Strengthens memory better, studies show, up to fifty percent improvement on tests (Roediger & Karpicke, 2006).

- **Interleaving, Mix You Must:** Different problems or subjects together study, it improves problem-solving and knowledge transfer (Taylor & Rohrer, 2010).

- **Dual Coding, Visual and Verbal Join Forces:** Combining words with images helps understanding and memory stick more firmly (Paivio, 1991).

- **Metacognition, Mindful You Become:** Awareness and self-checking of learning you do—improves outcome it does (Schraw & Dennison, 1994).

Strong, the evidence stands. Dunlosky and friends (2013) found distributed and retrieval practices highest in utility. Use these techniques, learners scored on average twenty-five percent higher, yes, than those who used common, less effective methods.

## 3. Core Concepts, Grasp Them You Should

- **Distributed Practice (Spacing),** spaced study sessions lead to memory building over days, not hours.  
- **Retrieval Practice (Testing Effect),** recall made active, by flashcards or self-quizzing, memory you strengthen.  
- **Interleaved Practice,** mix subjects or topics in practice, isolate not like blocks you must.  
- **Elaboration,** explain details, connect new with old knowledge, deepen understanding you do.  
- **Dual Coding,** use verbal and visual info together, deeper learning results.  
- **Metacognition,** plan, monitor, evaluate your learning journey.  
- **Concrete Examples,** specific cases, grasp abstract ideas easier they make.  
- **Avoid Passive Review,** highlighting or just reading, little benefit they bring.

## 5. Common Misconceptions, Clear Them We Must

- **“Cramming equals distributed study?”** False this is. Short-term help, cramming may bring, but long-term loss it causes.  
- **“Highlighting and re-reading best ways?”** Passive methods these are. Active recall and spacing more powerful.  
- **“Learning styles must be matched?”** Little empirical support there is; multiple modes (dual coding) better serve most learners.  
- **“More hours studied, always better?”** Quality and strategy count more than time alone.  
- **“Multitasking aids study?”** Focus lost it is; retention suffers strongly.

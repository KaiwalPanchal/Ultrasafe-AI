## 1. Overview

This style guide outlines how to craft email newsletters that resonate with a Millennial audience while keeping the content accessible and easy to understand for beginners (newbies). ## 3. Format-Specific Guidelines

### Structure Requirements
- **Subject Line:** Short, catchy, and benefit-driven (max 6-8 words).
- **Preheader:** Teaser text, engaging and complementary to subject.
- **Header:** Clear newsletter title or greeting.
- **Body:** 
  - Welcome/intro paragraph
  - Main content broken into sections with subheadings
  - Call-to-action (CTA) highlighted toward the end
- **Footer:** Contact info, unsubscribe link, social links.

### Length Considerations
- Total content length: approx. 300â€“500 words.
- Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences).
- Avoid overwhelming readers; space content with white space or line breaks.

### Visual Elements
- Include relevant, friendly images or icons aligned with the tone.
- Use clear, legible fonts (sans-serif preferred).
- Add dividers or spacing to separate sections visually.
- Minimal but consistent use of brand colors.

### Platform-Specific Considerations
- Optimize for mobile viewing: responsive layout, short lines.
- Test subject and preheader length for popular email clients.
- Ensure alt text for images for accessibility.

## 4. Complexity Adaptations

### How to Adjust Technical Depth
- Strip down complex concepts to their core ideas.
- Use analogies relatable to daily life.
- Provide brief explanations with links to deeper resources if needed.

### Vocabulary Considerations
- Replace advanced terms with simpler synonyms.
- Define any necessary technical words in simple language immediately after usage.

### Explanation Strategies
- Use step-by-step instructions or lists.
- Reinforce key points with examples or scenarios.
- Avoid assumptions about prior knowledge.
